01
The Concept

Matrix Pokémon is a tactical evolution of the **Pokémon Trading Card Game**. In this version, your deck is your battlefield. Almost all cards are placed face-down in a grid – the Matrix.

Standard Rule Alignment: Unless specified in this Matrix guide, all standard Pokémon TCG rules (Energy requirements, Weakness/Resistance, Abilities, and Special Conditions) remain active and apply during combat.

Core Hardware

  • 2 Standard Decks (60 cards each)
  • 2 Coins (Markers & Flips)
  • Damage Dice (1 dot = 10 damage)
  • Wide Table (2 grids of 9x6)
02
System Preparation
  1. The Prize Reserve: Shuffle your 60-card deck. Set **6 Prize Cards** face-down to the side.
  2. Matrix Initialization: Lay out the remaining **54 cards** in a **9x6 grid** (9 columns, 6 rows) face-down.
  1. Startup Reveal: Reveal **7 cards** of your choice. You **must** reveal at least one **Basic Pokémon**.
    If no Basic Pokémon is found, reshuffle everything and restart.
  2. Active Designation: Mark one revealed Basic Pokémon with a coin. This is your **Active Pokémon**.
  3. The Coin Flip: Flip a coin to determine Player 1.
03
Turn Protocols

1. Reveal Phase (Draw Phase Replacement)

Instead of drawing cards into a hand, you reveal face-down cards in your Matrix to open paths.

Player TurnAction
Player 1 (Turn 1)Reveal 0 cards (Advantage balance)
Player 2 (Turn 1)Reveal 1 card
All Further TurnsReveal 1 card

2. Move & Action Phase

Every revealed Pokémon can move **once per turn**. Pokémon move like the **Queen in Chess** (straight or diagonal, any distance) but only over **revealed spaces**. Face-down cards act as impassable walls.

  • Energy: Collect by landing on it (max 1/turn).
  • Evolution: Place on top when landing on matching card.
  • Trainers: Activate immediately by passing over.

3. Attack Phase

Your Active Pokémon attacks the opponent's Active Pokémon using standard Energy costs.

04
Combat & Distance Rule
DMG = BASE - (YOUR ROW-1)x10 - (ENEMY ROW-1)x10
Row 1: -0
Row 2: -10
Row 3: -20
Row 4: -30
Row 5: -40
Row 6: -50

Positioning is tactical: Row 1 is high-power/high-risk. Row 6 is max safety.

Knock Out (KO) Sequence

  1. Defeated Pokémon and all attached cards go to the **Discard Pile**.
  2. The winner takes a **Prize Card** and places it **face-up** in an empty spot in their Matrix.
    Pokémon V/ex are worth 2 Prize Cards.
  3. Immediately choose a new Active Pokémon from your revealed Basic cards.
05
Matrix Search Protocol

When a Trainer card (e.g. Great Ball) allows searching the deck:

  1. Scan: Pick up all cards one by one (Top-Left to Bottom-Right, row by row).
  2. Search: Find the desired card.
  3. Re-Initialize: Shuffle the remaining cards and re-lay them **face-down** in the grid.
  4. Deploy: Place the searched card **face-up** in any empty spot of your choice.

06
Strategy Tips

Defensive Anchoring

Keep your backup attackers in Row 6 to minimize incoming damage while they charge up.

Path Clearing

Prioritize revealing cards that create diagonal corridors for your "Queen" movement.

Prize Deployment

Place face-up Prize cards in the center of your matrix to increase your revealed "Road" network.

Evolution Bait

Lure the opponent forward into Row 1, then strike from a safe distance using a Stage 2 Pokémon.
