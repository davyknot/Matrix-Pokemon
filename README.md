## 🌟 What is Matrix Pokémon?

Matrix Pokémon is a **fan card game** that combines **Pokémon** with **Chess**.

All 54 cards are laid face-down in a grid – the **Matrix**. You reveal cards, move your Pokémon across the field, collect Energy, and battle your opponent.

The key twist: **Your position on the Matrix determines how hard you hit!**
The closer your Pokémon is to the opponent, the more damage it deals. The further back the defending Pokémon stands, the more it is protected.

---

## 🎒 What You Need

Each player needs:

- **1 Pokémon deck** with exactly **60 cards**
- **1 coin** (to mark the active Pokémon & for coin flips)
- **Dice** as damage counters *(1 pip = 10 HP damage)*
- **Table space** large enough for two Matrices (9 × 6 cards each)

---

## 🏆 Goal of the Game

Collect **6 Prize Cards** – and you win!

You earn a Prize Card every time you knock out one of your opponent's Pokémon.
**Pokémon V, ex, GX**, etc. give **2 Prize Cards** (same as regular Pokémon).

## 🛠️ Setup

### Step 1 – Shuffle
Shuffle your 60-card deck thoroughly.

### Step 2 – Set aside Prize Cards
Place **6 cards** face-down to the side – these are your **Prize Cards**. Don't look at them!

### Step 3 – Lay out the Matrix

Lay the remaining **54 cards** in a grid:
- **6 rows** of **9 cards** each
- All cards **face-down**

```
        ↑ ↑ ↑   OPPONENT'S SIDE   ↑ ↑ ↑

Row 1   [C][C][C][C][C][C][C][C][C]  ← Front Line (close to opponent)
Row 2   [C][C][C][C][C][C][C][C][C]
Row 3   [C][C][C][C][C][C][C][C][C]
Row 4   [C][C][C][C][C][C][C][C][C]
Row 5   [C][C][C][C][C][C][C][C][C]
Row 6   [C][C][C][C][C][C][C][C][C]  ← Back Row (close to you)

        ↓ ↓ ↓   YOUR SIDE         ↓ ↓ ↓
```

> 💡 **Row 1** = Front Line = maximum attack power, but no protection!
> **Row 6** = Back Row = well protected, but weaker attacks.

### Step 4 – Reveal Starting Cards

Each player reveals **7 cards** – you choose freely which ones.

⚠️ **Important:** At least **1 Basic Pokémon** must be among them!

> If no Basic Pokémon is revealed:
> → Flip all cards back over, re-shuffle and re-lay your Matrix. Reveal again.

> 💡 Tip: Pick cards that are next to each other – that way your Pokémon can immediately walk toward its Energy!

### Step 5 – Set Your Active Pokémon

Place a **coin** on one of your revealed Basic Pokémon – that is your **active Pokémon**.

### Step 6 – Who Goes First?

Flip a coin. Whoever gets **heads** is **Player 1** and goes first.

---

## 🔄 Your Turn – Step by Step

Players alternate turns. Each turn has **4 steps**:

```
  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  1. Reveal Cards  →  2. Move Pokémon            │
  │  3. Play Trainers →  4. Attack                  │
  └──────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

### Step 1: Reveal Cards (Draw Phase)

Instead of drawing cards, you **reveal cards** from your Matrix:

| Situation | Cards to Reveal |
|-----------|----------------|
| Player 1, **1st turn** | **0 cards** – compensation for going first |
| Player 2, **1st turn** | **1 card** – small compensation |
| **All other turns** | **1 card** |

You choose **freely** which cards to reveal.

> 💡 Think carefully! Every revealed card opens new paths for your Pokémon – but also for your opponent!

---

### Step 2: Move Pokémon

**Order of movement:**

1. Move your **active Pokémon** first (if you want to move it)
2. Then move all **other Pokémon** (each one once, optional)
3. Pokémon may also **stay put** – you don't have to move

Each Pokémon may move **at most once per turn** and **at most 4 spaces**.

#### How does a Pokémon move?

Pokémon move like the **Queen in chess**:
- Straight (up / down / left / right) **or** diagonally
- **At most 4 spaces** in one direction (no changing direction mid-move)
- **Only over revealed cards** – face-down cards are walls!

```
          ↖  ↑  ↗
           \ | /
      ← ←   [🔥]   → →
           / | \
          ↙  ↓  ↘

  [🔥] = your Pokémon
   Arrows = possible directions (max. 4 spaces)
```

#### Where may a Pokémon stop?

| Card Type | Allowed? | What happens? |
|-----------|----------|---------------|
| **Energy Card** | ✅ Yes | Attach 1 Energy to the Pokémon |
| **Matching Evolution Card** | ✅ Yes | Pokémon evolves immediately |
| **Free revealed space** | ✅ Yes | Pokémon simply stands there |
| **Trainer Card** | ⚠️ Choice | Pokémon passes OVER it; you choose whether to play it |
| **Another Pokémon** | ❌ No | Two Pokémon can never share a space |

> ⚠️ **Note:** Stage 1 and Stage 2 Evolution cards lying alone on the Matrix **cannot move on their own**. They sit like items on the field until a matching Basic Pokémon walks onto them.

---

### Step 3: Play Trainer and Item Cards

| Card Type | How often per turn? |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Item Card** (e.g. Potion, Great Ball) | As many times as you like |
| **Supporter Card** (e.g. Professor's Research) | **Once** per turn |

Played cards go to the **discard pile**.

---

### Step 4: Attack

Your active Pokémon may **attack once** (optional).

> 🚫 **Important:** Player 1 **may not attack** on their very first turn!

The attack's strength depends on the defending Pokémon's position → see **Battle & Damage**.

---

## ⚡ Collecting Energy

When a Pokémon lands on an Energy Card:
- The Energy is placed **under the Pokémon** (attached as normal)
- Each Pokémon can collect **1 Energy Card per turn**
- This applies to **all** your Pokémon on the Matrix – not just the active one!

> 💡 Tip: Move your non-active Pokémon toward Energy Cards so they charge up while your active Pokémon fights!

---

## 🔼 Evolution

When your Pokémon lands on a **matching Evolution Card**:

1. Place the Evolution Card **on top of** the Pokémon
2. The evolved Pokémon immediately has new attacks and more HP
3. A Pokémon can evolve **once per turn**

---

## ⚔️ Battle & Damage

### The Key Rule: Position is Power!

The further **forward** (Row 1) the defending Pokémon is → no protection, full damage lands!
The further **back** (Row 6) the defending Pokémon is → armored, reduced damage!

### The Damage Formula

> **Effective Damage = Base Damage − (Defender's Row − 1) × 10**

Simple version for kids:
> "Every row the defending Pokémon is **behind the front** gives it **10 damage protection!**"

#### Protection by Row (Defender)

| Defender's Row | Protection | Example (Base Damage 60) |
|---------------|------------|--------------------------|
| Row 1 – Front | −0 | 60 damage lands |
| Row 2 | −10 | 50 damage lands |
| Row 3 | −20 | 40 damage lands |
| Row 4 | −30 | 30 damage lands |
| Row 5 | −40 | 20 damage lands |
| Row 6 – Back | −50 | 10 damage lands |

> ⚠️ Damage can **never go below 0**!

#### Examples

| Attacker | Defender | Base Damage | Protection | Effective Damage |
|----------|---------|------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Anywhere | Row 1 | 20 | −0 | **20** |
| Anywhere | Row 2 | 20 | −10 | **10** |
| Anywhere | Row 3 | 20 | −20 | **0** |
| Anywhere | Row 2 | 80 | −10 | **70** |
| Anywhere | Row 6 | 100 | −50 | **50** |

### Tracking Damage with Dice

Place dice on the damaged Pokémon:
**1 pip = 10 HP damage**

Example: 30 effective damage → place 3 dice on the Pokémon.

---

## 💀 Knocked Out Pokémon

A Pokémon is **knocked out** when its accumulated damage equals or exceeds its HP.

**What happens:**

1. The knocked-out Pokémon + all its attached Energy → **discard pile** (loser's)
2. The winner takes **1 Prize Card** *(Pokémon V, ex, GX → **2 Prize Cards**)*
3. The Prize Card goes on the **winner's own Prize Stack** beside the Matrix *(not back on the Matrix!)*
4. The loser immediately picks a new **active Pokémon** → choose a revealed Basic Pokémon
5. No revealed Basic Pokémon? → Reveal cards until one appears

## 🔍 Searching Your Library

When a Trainer Card lets you search your deck (= search your Matrix):

**The Snake Pattern:**

```
Start TOP-LEFT:
→ → → → → → → → →   (Row 1: left to right)
← ← ← ← ← ← ← ← ←   (Row 2: right to left)
→ → → → → → → → →   (Row 3: left to right)
... continue until the target card is found.
```

**How to do it:**

1. Start **top-left** in your Matrix
2. Reveal cards in the snake pattern (↔), look at each one
3. As soon as you find the **target card**: **Stop!**
4. Shuffle all other revealed cards well
5. Re-lay them **starting top-left** in the snake pattern – **face-down**
6. The target card: place it **face-up** at a free spot of your choice (or use it immediately)

---

## 🗑️ Discard Pile

These cards go to the discard pile:

- Knocked-out Pokémon (including all attached Energy Cards)
- Played Trainer Cards
- Played Supporter Cards

---

## 🏁 End of Game

The game ends **immediately** when:

| Condition | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| A player takes their **6th Prize Card** | That player **wins!** 🏆 |
| A player has **no more Pokémon** on the Matrix | That player **loses** |

---

## 🧠 Strategy Tips

1. **Play many Basic Pokémon!** You need them for the initial reveal and as replacement active Pokémon.
2. **Front = Attack:** Move fully charged Pokémon to Row 1 for maximum damage.
3. **Back = Charge:** Safely charge up new Pokémon in the back rows.
4. **Reveal wisely:** Open paths for your Pokémon – but don't open new paths for your opponent!
5. **Plan Energy routes:** Move your Pokémon so they pick up Energy on the way to the front.
6. **Secure Evolutions:** Walk to your Evolution Card before your opponent blocks the way.
7. **4-space strategy:** Plan step by step – no Pokémon can jump from the very back to the front in one move!

## 📋 Quick Reference

| Phase | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 1. Reveal Cards | 3 cards (1st round: 0 for Player 1, 1 for Player 2) |
| 2. Move | Active Pokémon first; then all others (once each, max. 4 spaces) |
| 3. Trainers | Items: as often as you like · Supporter: once |
| 4. Attack | Once, optional (Player 1 cannot attack on turn 1) |

**Damage:** Base − (Defender's Row − 1) × 10 · Min. 0
**Win:** Take 6 Prize Cards **or** opponent has no Pokémon left

## 📖 Design Decisions (Rules Log)

### DE-101: Simplified Damage Formula
**Problem:** The original formula (deduction for BOTH Pokémon positions) creates 0-damage situations that are frustrating for young players.
**Decision:** Only the **defender's row** determines protection.
**Reason:** Works like "armor" – easy to explain, and there's always a chance to deal damage.

### DE-102: Maximum Movement of 4 Spaces
**Problem:** Unlimited movement (like the chess queen) lets a Pokémon jump from Row 6 to Row 1 in a single turn, making positional strategy meaningless.
**Decision:** Maximum **4 spaces** per turn.
**Reason:** Forces gradual advance; makes positioning matter.

### DE-103: Player 1 Cannot Attack on Turn 1
**Problem:** Player 1 already has the advantage of going first.
**Decision:** Player 1 **cannot attack** on their very first turn.
**Reason:** Standard Pokémon rule; balances the first-mover advantage.

### DE-104: Trainer Cards – Player Chooses
**Problem:** Auto-triggering a Trainer Card when passing over it creates edge cases (e.g. if a Supporter was already played).
**Decision:** When a Pokémon passes over a Trainer Card, the **player may choose**: play it now OR leave it face-up on the Matrix.
**Reason:** Gives the player control; avoids ambiguous situations.

### DE-105: Prize Cards Go to Own Stack
**Problem:** Putting won Prize Cards back on the Matrix is confusing.
**Decision:** Won Prize Cards go on a **separate winner's stack** beside the Matrix.
**Reason:** Cleaner; no follow-up questions (e.g. what if the Prize Card is a Pokémon?).

### DE-106: Row Numbering
**Decision:** **Row 1** = Front Line = close to opponent = no protection. **Row 6** = Back Row = close to you = full protection.
**Reason:** Moving forward = more damage; moving back = more protection.
